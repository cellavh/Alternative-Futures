Alternative Futures

Since I was a kid, I have always been someone with many hobbies and interests. It was hard for me to focus on one thing, because I always wanted to try new things.

Sometimes, I imagined myself becoming an inventor, creating things that could change the world.

Other times, I saw myself as a musician, lost in the melodies I composed.

There were days when I wanted to be a scientist, unraveling the mysteries of the universe.

And moments where I thought I could be a writer, crafting stories that transport people to different worlds.

The possibilities felt endless, and choosing just one felt impossible.

I remember a time when I tried to stick to just one thing. I thought, if I forced myself into a single path, life would be easier. But it wasn’t.

I studied hard, I chose a major, I committed to a career path. But something always felt missing.

I would watch documentaries about travelers and wonder what it would be like to explore new cultures.

I read about artists who made a living from their creativity and imagined what it would feel like to express myself freely.

I met people who had built careers in science and technology, and I admired their dedication to discovery.

Each dream tugged at me, whispering that there was more, that I didn’t have to choose just one thing.

I realized I didn’t want to be defined by a single title. Maybe I could be all of these things, in different ways, at different times.

So now, I embrace the uncertainty. The adventure of not knowing exactly where I will end up, but trusting that each step I take is part of something bigger.

Maybe the best future is the one I create, piece by piece, from all the things I love.
